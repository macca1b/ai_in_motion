Tableau navigating the AI boom

... How is our favourite data viz capability tooling up on the AI journey

Tableau is one of the most popular tools for turning raw data into interactive and visually appealing insights. It was founded in 2003 by a group of Stanford researchers who wanted to make data analysis easier and more intuitive. Over the years, it has grown massively, and after being acquired by Salesforce in 2019 for $15.7 billion, it's now used by millions of people worldwideâ€”from small businesses to Fortune 500 companies. Today, Tableau is stepping into the AI era with features like Tableau GPT and Einstein Discovery, which bring automation, natural language queries, and predictive analytics into the mix. This means users can get instant insights, ask questions in plain English, and make smarter decisions without needing deep technical skills. Let's take a dive into what is going on and what's ahead.

What's happening with Tableau?

AI in Tableau Accelerate your data culture with AI-powered insights.

AI Analytics is for everyone.

  • AI in Tableau democratizes data analysis and simplifies insights consumption at scale by bringing trusted generative AI to the entire Tableau platform.
  • This next generation of Tableau accelerates time to value and reduces repetitive tasks for the data analyst with smart suggestions and in-product guidance.
  • And because our AI is built on the Einstein Trust Layer, it allows administrators to enable trusted, ethical, and open AI-powered experiences without compromising data security and privacy.
  • AI in Tableau also empowers business users with smart, personalized, and contextualized insights in the flow of work.


    • AI in Tableau includes components Tableau Agent, Pulse, Next and the Einstein Trust layer... so lets break them down.

    Introduction of Tableau Agent (formerly Einstein Copilot)

    In mid-2024, Tableau introduced Tableau Agent, an AI-driven assistant designed to streamline data analysis tasks.

  • Supercharge analysts... get from data preparation to visualization faster with a trusted advisor right in the flow of work.
  • This feature allows users to interact with data using natural language, enabling conversational data exploration, guided calculation creation, and smart suggestions for data visualization. Learn more
  • Accelerate data-driven decision making with a trusted AI assistant.
  • With the power of Tableau Agent, new analysts can turn data into insights faster than ever.
  • Breeze through data preparation by describing calculations in natural language.
  • Effortlessly generate comprehensive descriptions for datasources, workbooks, and tables in just one click.
  • Create calculations and visualizations using conversational prompts, and get suggested questions to jumpstart data exploration.





    • What is Tableau Next?

    An advanced analytics platform built on the Salesforce Platform, Tableau Next integrates deeply with Agentforce to deliver personalized, contextual, and actionable insights across organizations.

  • It offers a flexible, API-first experience, enabling users to access insights seamlessly within their workflows.
  • Tableau Next is a reimagined analytics platform built on the Salesforce Platform, designed to transform agile, self-service analytics using reusable and extensible components, semantic AI, and consistent data workflows.
  • Benefits - Empowers users to make strategic decisions quickly by delivering AI-powered insights directly within their workflows, enhancing efficiency and data-driven actions.
  • Future Outlook - As AI technology evolves, Tableau Next is expected to further enhance its co-pilot capabilities, including more advanced predictive analytics, deeper integration with other AI tools, and enhanced automation features, making data analysis more intuitive and efficient.

    • Launch of Tableau Pulse

    Empower business users... Gain distilled, meaningful insights from all your data to guide every decision.

  • Tableau Pulse offers a reimagined data experience by providing personalized and contextual insights directly within users' workflows.
  • It delivers automated analytics in plain language, anticipates user questions, and suggests new avenues for data exploration, thereby transforming how users engage with their data. Discover Tableau Pulse
  • Powered by AI, Tableau Pulse is a reimagined data experience for business users that empowers every employee with smart, personalized, and contextual insights delivered directly in their flow of work.
  • Tableau Pulse surfaces automated analytics in plain language, proactively anticipates user questions and even suggests questions they might not have thought of otherwise.
  • Tableau Pulse transforms how people engage with their data, helping everyone in an organization put data at the center of every decision.

    • Tableau Einstein Trust Layer

    Build on a trusted foundation... leverage all the productivity gains of generative AI without compromising on data security and privacy.

  • AI in Tableau is built on the Einstein Trust Layer.
  • This trust layer protects the privacy and safety of your data so you can unleash the power of AI without compromising on your security standards.
  • The layer is within the AI Cloud, which sets a new industry standard for trusted enterprise AI offering the benefits of generative AI securely.
  • The Einstein Trust Layer also makes AI Cloud open and extensible, optimizing the right model for the right task, while offering customer choice.

    Future Outlook...

  • As AI technology continues to evolve, Tableau is expected to further enhance its co-pilot capabilities.
  • Future developments may include more advanced predictive analytics, deeper integration with other AI tools, and enhanced automation features, all aimed at making data analysis more intuitive and efficient.
  • These AI-driven enhancements signify Tableau's commitment to evolving its platform, ensuring users can harness the full potential of their data with greater ease and confidence.
    • Note: In 2019, Salesforce, a global leader in customer relationship management (CRM), acquired Tableau, a prominent data visualization company, in an all-stock deal valued at $15.7 billion. This acquisition merged Salesforce's robust CRM capabilities with Tableau's advanced data analytics and visualization tools, offering users enhanced insights and a more comprehensive understanding of customer data.
