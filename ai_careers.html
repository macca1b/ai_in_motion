The Problem

Everyone’s talking about how AI will change jobs. But what does that actually look like for a specific role — a data analyst, a nurse, a teacher, a copywriter? This tool tries to answer that.

How It Works

We take real job listings, break them down into core skills, then map how those skills might evolve, stay relevant, or become obsolete as AI progresses. This isn’t just about automation — it’s about augmentation, new opportunities, and where to focus your learning next.

Who It's For

If you’re working, hiring, learning, or teaching — this project is for you. It’s built to help professionals reskill, students figure out what to study, and organisations rethink talent in an age of constant disruption.

The Big Picture

Most of the AI conversation is too zoomed out — all stats, no texture. This tool zooms in. Role by role, skill by skill. And from those details, bigger patterns begin to emerge. It’s not a crystal ball. But it is a way to ask better questions about the future of work.

About Me

I'm a data analyst by trade, a writer by instinct, and someone who's always been drawn to the edges — where technology, creativity, and culture blur into each other.

I’ve spent years building systems, leading teams, and asking tough questions about how we use data. Lately, I’ve been rethinking all of it through the lens of AI — not just what these tools can do, but what they mean for how we work, learn, and live.

This site is my attempt to capture that exploration. Not polished answers — just honest thinking, in progress.
