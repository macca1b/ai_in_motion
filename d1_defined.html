AI Defined...

In the fast-moving world of AI, it can feel like new terms, acronyms, and buzzwords pop up every week — and keeping up can be overwhelming. Some concepts are well-established, while others like prompt engineering, foundation models, or AGI are still taking shape. That’s why this space has been created: to help cut through jargon and stay grounded in what really matters.

Here, you’ll find clear, concise definitions of key terms in the AI space — whether you’re a curious newcomer, a tech-savvy marketer, or building with AI yourself. But we go a step further. We add context, real-world examples, recent news and tools to see how these subjects are playing out in the wild. Whether exploring AI for the first time or brushing up on the latest shifts, this is your go-to AI glossary.

Definitions

AGI

'Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is AI that’s as smart and flexible as a human. It can learn anything you can, solve new problems it’s never seen before, and understand the world — not just do one thing like playing chess or generating pictures.'

  • More

Quantum Computing

'a type of computing that uses principles of quantum mechanics to process information. Unlike classical computers, which use bits (0 or 1), quantum computers use quantum bits (qubits) — which can represent 0, 1, or both at the same time through a property called superposition. This allows quantum computers to perform calculations exponentially faster, especially in fields like cryptography, chemistry and AI.'

  • More

Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI)

'ASI is AI that’s way smarter than any human ever could be — not just at math or memory, but in thinking, inventing, making decisions, and understanding the world. It would be like comparing a human to a mouse — we just wouldn’t be able to keep up.'
