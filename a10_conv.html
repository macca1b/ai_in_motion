Conversational AI - what's being said?

Lets take look

AI is increasingly positioned as a copilot in various workflows, playing a crucial role in enhancing efficiency, accelerating processes, and handling routine tasks with greater speed. Many marketers already integrate AI into their daily operations, leveraging its capabilities to refine strategies, automate workflows, and improve overall productivity. However, AI as a copilot extends beyond customer-facing applications or high-profile marketing campaigns. Some of the most impactful AI-driven improvements happen internally, optimizing operations behind the scenes. The question is whether AI is being fully utilized in these areas or if untapped opportunities for efficiency remain.

Internal AI Copilot Use Cases

  • Unilever (B2C Consumer Goods) – The company implemented Alex, a custom GPT-3 assistant, to assist consumer care and marketing teams with email management. Alex processes incoming emails, detects sentiment and intent, and drafts responses in the correct brand voice. This led to a 90% reduction in response time, significantly improving productivity. Additionally, another AI tool, Homer, automates the creation of product descriptions, demonstrating AI's role in streamlining internal content generation.
  • Bloomreach (B2B SaaS) – To scale its content marketing efforts, Bloomreach equipped its four-person team with an AI writing assistant (Jasper). By automating first drafts and repetitive writing tasks, the team achieved a 113% increase in blog output and a 40% boost in organic traffic. This allowed them to expand content distribution without increasing headcount while focusing on high-level SEO strategy and creative work.
  • Buzet (Marketing Agency) – A small agency leveraged the AI platform Scalenut as a research and copy assistant, automating time-consuming SEO research, content outlines, and draft generation. This resulted in a 25% increase in organic traffic, demonstrating how lean teams can maximize output by using AI to handle foundational tasks more efficiently.
  • Ally Financial (B2C Banking) – The company developed Ally.ai, a proprietary GPT-powered assistant designed to assist the marketing team with planning and content-related tasks. During a one-month pilot, the AI delivered a 34% reduction in time spent on routine marketing tasks, translating into nearly 3,000 work hours saved annually. This efficiency gain enables the team to reallocate time toward strategic and creative work.
  • Influencer Marketing Factory (B2B Agency) – By integrating an AI-driven outreach copilot through the platform Reply, the agency automated PR and link-building emails. This resulted in over 70 press mentions in major publications such as The Wall Street Journal and Adweek, with minimal manual effort. AI handled contact discovery and follow-ups, acting as an extended team member to significantly expand reach.

AI’s Role in Internal Productivity

Beyond marketing and customer engagement, AI serves as a valuable copilot for everyday internal tasks:

  • Idea Generator & Brainstorming Assistant – AI can provide fresh perspectives on internal projects, meeting agendas, and even complex workplace discussions.
  • Process Automation – Tasks such as data relabeling, reformatting, and content restructuring, often assigned to interns or junior staff, can now be automated seamlessly.
  • Project Planning & Task Management – Platforms like Asana integrate AI to generate project roadmaps instantly, offering a structured starting point that can be refined as needed.
  • Documentation & Knowledge Management – AI converts recorded meetings or instructional videos into structured Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), simplifying documentation efforts.

From handling minor repetitive tasks to restructuring entire workflows, AI is not just an external marketing tool—it is an indispensable asset for boosting internal efficiency, workflow organization, and long-term productivity.

The Zendesk book on getting your AI copilot-ready


1. Audit your existing operations

Evaluate your operations to identify where copilots can provide immediate value. That includes existing friction points, but also high-volume, complex interactions that still need a human touch. Start by examining your macros and collaborating with agents to uncover cases that frequently require editing, personalization, or customization. Review your ticket data to identify queries with high resolution times, as these are prime opportunities for copilots to streamline workflows. Your quality assurance (QA) data is another goldmine of insights. Use it to pinpoint areas where agents struggle following your business procedures or high-touch scenarios that require more guidance over their responses. These learnings can guide where copilots can drive quick wins and deliver fast ROI.


2. Treat copilots like a new teammate

Just as you onboard new hires with detailed training on your business and workflows, your copilots benefit from the same thoughtful preparation. Start by updating your existing documentation to reflect current processes. Optimize it for AI by using concise, straightforward language to define your business logic, ensuring copilots can access and interpret information quickly and accurately. Break multi-step actions into clear, sequential steps to ensure the copilot processes each task correctly without confusion. While admins are essential for drafting CX documentation, this is another area where involving your agents is essential. They often hold untapped knowledge that hasn’t been formalized yet and firsthand insight into the latest procedures, making them essential for identifying gaps and updates. Plus, involving them in this process offers an opportunity to upskill your agents, empowering them to play a larger role in shaping your AI strategy.


3. Gather feedback early

Think of AI adoption as a marathon, not a sprint. Pilot a few of the use cases you identified in step one to gather valuable feedback before expanding to more advanced applications. A structured feedback loop is key—encourage agents to leave real-time notes in your CX system, and leverage tools like Slack channels or quick survey forms for continuous input. Don’t stop there. Speak directly with your agents to understand their experience firsthand. Ask how often they’re making edits, what types of changes they’re making and why, and where they see room for improvement. Your ultimate goal is for your agents to confidently and consistently hit “approve,” making AI a trusted partner in their workflow.


4. Create a plan for continuous improvement

AI isn’t a “set it and forget it” solution. As your processes change, make it a priority to refresh your copilot scripts and internal documentation it relies on. Regular updates keep copilots accurate and reliable, ensuring they remain a trusted partner for your team. Lean into QA tools to measure quality improvements over time. Focus on key metrics like customer satisfaction, average handle time, and time to resolution to capture success. Tag critical use cases to accurately track and analyze ticket data, giving you actionable insights to continuously refine and optimize your strategy.


5. Find opportunities for copilots to take the lead

Copilots don’t just guide agents through resolutions. They can also handle repetitive tasks independently, with 85 percent of CX leaders agreeing that copiloting is a critical first step towards autonomous AI. From processing refunds to updating customer details, copilots lighten your agents’ workload so they can focus on what truly matters: building deeper customer relationships. Start by identifying areas where copilots can take direct action to save your agents even more time. That includes managing order changes, transferring data across systems, or other routine tasks. This requires seamlessly integrating copilots with your core business systems using secure, scalable middleware—proactively engage your IT and security teams early in the process to secure approvals and avoid roadblocks later.

The Impact of AI Copilots in Business and Marketing in Early 2025

As of early 2025, AI copilots have moved beyond simple task automation and are now deeply embedded in business and marketing workflows. Companies across industries are using AI-driven assistants to streamline operations, enhance decision-making, and significantly reduce manual workload. In marketing, AI copilots are transforming content generation, audience segmentation, and campaign optimization—allowing brands to scale personalized messaging with unprecedented efficiency. Businesses are also leveraging copilots for customer service, using AI-powered chatbots and email responders that handle inquiries with near-human accuracy, reducing response times and increasing customer satisfaction. The rapid adoption of AI copilots has led to notable gains in productivity, with small teams achieving what previously required much larger workforces. In sectors like finance, legal, and product development, AI copilots assist with data analysis, research, and even regulatory compliance, cutting costs while improving accuracy.


The Likely Direction for AI Copilots in 2025

Throughout the rest of 2025, AI copilots are expected to become even more autonomous and integrated into strategic decision-making processes. Businesses will likely shift from viewing AI as a tool for task completion to considering it an integral collaborator in high-level planning. Advancements in AI-human interaction will make copilots more intuitive, allowing them to anticipate business needs and suggest proactive solutions. Marketing teams will increasingly rely on AI to generate creative assets, automate A/B testing, and dynamically adjust campaigns in real time based on audience behavior. AI copilots will also reshape operations by integrating seamlessly into enterprise resource planning (ERP) and customer relationship management (CRM) systems, ensuring that data flows efficiently across departments. In addition, ethical and regulatory concerns surrounding AI usage will become a focal point, with governments and businesses refining policies to balance innovation with accountability.


Beyond 2025: The Future of AI Copilots in Business and Workforce Transformation

Looking further ahead, AI copilots are poised to redefine business structures by automating not just repetitive tasks but also complex decision-making processes. In industries such as manufacturing, AI-driven copilots will coordinate supply chains, predict demand fluctuations, and optimize production schedules, minimizing waste and increasing efficiency. In creative fields, AI may take on roles as co-designers, developing branding concepts, ad scripts, and even video content with minimal human input. The potential for AI to replace jobs will depend on the industry; while some roles—such as data entry, routine customer service, and administrative tasks—will become largely automated, other jobs will evolve to focus on strategic oversight and innovation. Rather than eliminating the workforce, AI copilots are expected to act as force multipliers, allowing businesses to scale without increasing headcount dramatically. The companies that embrace AI as a collaborative partner rather than a replacement tool will likely gain the greatest competitive advantage in the years ahead.
